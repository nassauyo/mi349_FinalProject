The "Push" portion of the PPL workout is designed to work the upper body muscles that do the "pushing". These muscles are chest, triceps, and shoulders. Unlike other types of workouts, you are activating muscle groups that work together in natural motion, instead of isolating one muscle.

Flat Dumbbell Bench Press

This exercise works your chest muscles and triceps.

Dumbbell Incline Chest Press

This exercise works your upper pectroalis major (upper chest) and triceps.

Barbell Bench Press

This exercise works your chest, shoulders, and triceps while locking your arms, making the up and down motion more controlled.

Arnold Dumbbell Press

This exercise works your shoulders and your triceps.

Standing Barbell Military Press

Similar to the barbell bench press, this exercise works your upper pectoralis major (upper chest), triceps, and shoulders.

Dumbbell Pullover

This exercise works your chest, triceps, and side muscles (Latissimus Dorsi).
